Frequently

When choosing a condom, consider factors like size, material (latex, polyisoprene, or polyurethane), lubrication, and texture. Ensuring the right fit helps improve comfort and effectiveness.
Lubricants reduce friction and enhance pleasure. Water-based lubricants are safe for all condoms, while silicone-based ones last longer. Avoid oil-based lubricants with latex condoms as they can degrade the material.
PrEP (Pre-exposure prophylaxis) is a daily pill that reduces the risk of HIV. PEP (Post-exposure prophylaxis) is taken after potential exposure to HIV and must be started within 72 hours.
Always use condoms correctly, choose high-quality lubricants, and regularly test for STIs. Using barriers like dental dams during oral sex can further enhance protection.
